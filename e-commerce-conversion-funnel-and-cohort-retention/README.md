## 📊 Project 2: E-Commerce Conversion Funnel & Cohort Retention Analysis
In this project, I analyzed raw user activity logs from an e-commerce platform to evaluate how effectively the website converts visitors into customers and how well the business retains those customers over time. I was tasked with answering the following business questions:

- How effectively do product page views convert into purchases?
- What percentage of users progress through each stage of the purchasing funnel?
- How well does the business retain customers after their first purchase?

To answer these questions, I transformed raw event logs into business metrics by constructing a conversion funnel and performing cohort analysis on customer purchases. I filtered and structured the dataset, calculated first purchase dates for each user, and tracked retention rates across monthly acquisition cohorts.

### 📉 Key Findings
- Approximately 29% of users who viewed products added items to their cart, while about 10% of viewers ultimately completed a purchase.  
- The largest drop-off occurs between product views and cart additions, indicating the primary friction point in the purchasing journey.
- Customer retention declines rapidly after the first purchase, with very low repeat purchase rates across subsequent cohort months, suggesting an opportunity to improve post-purchase engagement and retention strategies.
